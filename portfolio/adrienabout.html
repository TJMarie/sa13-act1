Who Am I?

adrien is a queer, Black and Korean indie artist based in Memphis, Tennessee. They specialize in abstract visual art and the composition and performance of alternative music. Being classically trained in piano and violin, they have branched out to guitar and bass guitar in order to shift towards alternative rock. "I, Paranoia", their first release, was conceived, written, performed, sung, and produced by one person.

Inspiration

Genetic depression and anxiety have been a lifelong struggle for the artist, having endured obsessive suicidal thoughts and hospitalizations for attempts on their own life. After being involuntarily committed, they were forced to grapple with the permanence of death, the reality of life, and the responsibility of having loved ones.

As a result, they made a resolution to continue with the rat-race and use their life to improve others'. Though Depression will likely always be a struggle, the goal is to make it an afterthought, seeking education and accomplishments to place at the forefront. In this corrupt, American justice system, the plan is to use a career in law to represent minority populations whose voices are too often disregarded.

Art

In the meantime, let us create art for the sake of art. One of adrien's strongest beliefs is that the creative pursuit is one of the greatest coping mechanisms available.

In one form or another, art is accessible to anyone, regardless of physical or mental ability. Much unlike traditional therapy (which undoubtedly has its place), conceptualizing and producing art is nearly free, which is why our society should be emphasizing and allocating resources towards artistic programs in schools. Instead, art programs across the country are being gutted while police forces and the military budget grow increasingly overpowered.

Being true to the authentic self, retaining critical thinking, and questioning everything is paramount to improve oneself, and are hallmarks of a great artist. I strive never to forget that.
